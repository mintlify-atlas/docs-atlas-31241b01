Research and discovery produce a rain of signals — links, tools, repos, findings — that are genuinely useful in the moment but typically lost within days. Decision Rain Library Project is a lightweight template for organizing these signals so they remain usable when you actually need them.

## The Signal Loss Problem

Bookmarks get forgotten. Saved links lose the context that made them worth saving. Discovered tools become unknown again two weeks later. Without a structure that captures *why* something was saved alongside *what* it is, the signal is effectively lost — you're left with a list of URLs you can't act on.

<Note>
  A link without context is just noise. The Decision Rain Library preserves the "why" alongside the "what."
</Note>

## Library Structure

Each entry in the library captures the information needed to act on a signal later — not just to recognize it.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Discovery date">
    When the signal was found. Provides temporal context and helps with periodic review.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Source">
    Where it came from — a search, a recommendation, a project, a conversation.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Relevance summary">
    Why it was worth capturing. This is the most important field — without it, the entry is just a bookmark.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Status">
    Current state of the entry: reviewed, pending, or discarded. Keeps the library actionable rather than a passive archive.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Next action">
    What to do with it, if anything — integrate, share, revisit, or close out. Optional, but useful for entries with clear follow-through potential.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How to Use It

<Steps>
  <Step title="Clone or copy the template">
    Clone or copy the Decision Rain Library template from [GitHub](https://github.com/XxYouDeaDPunKxX/decision-rain-library-project).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up your library structure">
    The template provides a starting format. Adapt it to your workflow — the structure matters more than the exact tool you use to run it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Capture signals immediately with context">
    When you discover something worth keeping, add it right away with a relevance summary. Context fades fast — capture it at the moment of discovery.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Periodically review entries">
    Set a regular review cadence to promote useful entries, discard stale ones, and act on anything with a clear next step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use it as a decision reference, not just an archive">
    When making a decision or starting a new project, consult the library first. Signals you captured weeks ago may be exactly what you need now.
  </Step>
</Steps>
