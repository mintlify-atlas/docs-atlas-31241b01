As AI-assisted projects run, everything ends up in one place — decisions, open questions, constraints, handoff notes, ideas. Signal Rail is a lightweight structure that keeps each type of signal on its own track.

## The Collapse Problem

Without a routing structure, a live AI-assisted project document becomes an accumulation layer. Decisions and open questions occupy the same space. Constraints get buried under later material and stop being enforced. Handoff notes land wherever there was room at the time, making them nearly impossible to locate when the session resumes.

The problem is not that the information is missing. The problem is that everything is present but nothing is findable, and you cannot tell what is settled from what is still open.

<Note>
  One messy document is the default outcome of any unstructured AI-assisted project.
</Note>

## The Five Signal Tracks

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Decisions">
    Finalized choices that should not be re-opened without a deliberate decision to do so. A decision logged on this track is treated as settled. If something here needs to change, the change is explicit and logged — not a quiet override.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Open Work">
    Tasks currently in flight or waiting for action. This track makes the active workload visible and separates it from ideas that haven't been committed to yet.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Ideas">
    Potential directions that have not been committed to. Keeping ideas on their own track prevents them from drifting into the decisions track or being treated as open work prematurely.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Constraints">
    Things that cannot change and must be respected throughout the project. Constraints belong on a dedicated track so they stay visible instead of being buried as the document grows.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Handoff Notes">
    Context needed to resume or transfer the project. This track is the resume point — the first thing read at the start of a new session or when ownership changes.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How to Use Signal Rail

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create the Signal Rail document">
    At the start of your project, create a Signal Rail document with all five tracks present. The structure exists before work begins, not after it has already collapsed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Route material as it arrives">
    As work runs, route each piece of material to the correct track when it first appears. Decisions go to decisions. Open items go to open work. Do not batch this at the end of a session.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review tracks at session boundaries">
    At the close of each session, review all five tracks. Promote ideas to open work or decisions if the session produced that outcome. Archive completed open work. Keep constraints current.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use handoff notes as your resume point">
    When starting a new session or handing off the project, open the handoff notes track first. This is the canonical entry point — not the top of a long scrolled document.
  </Step>
</Steps>
