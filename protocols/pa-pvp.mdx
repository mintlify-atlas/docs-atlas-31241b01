PA-PVP is a PLUTONIUM-like adversarial peer validation protocol. The name comes from the review structure: one AI challenges, another defends, and the protocol forces a real verdict out of the process. It was built for the specific failure mode where AI-assisted review keeps producing more open questions, more discussion threads, and more things to consider — but never a decision. PA-PVP closes that loop. You submit a plan, artifact, or stuck decision; the protocol runs it through an adversarial cycle; you get a verdict, one next action, and a point to continue from.

## The Problem It Solves

Standard AI review is additive. Ask an AI to review something and it surfaces concerns, suggests improvements, raises edge cases, and offers alternatives — all useful things that also leave you with more work and no clear answer. The session ends with a longer to-do list, not a verdict.

<Warning>
  Endless AI back-and-forth without a verdict is a workflow dead end. PA-PVP
  closes the loop.
</Warning>

PA-PVP is adversarial by design. The protocol forces the review to produce a decision, not a discussion. If the work passes, you know why. If it fails, you know what to fix. If it's conditional, you know the exact condition.

## Protocol Outputs

Every PA-PVP run produces exactly three outputs:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Verdict" icon="gavel">
    Pass, fail, or conditional. Not "it depends" — a real verdict with the
    reasoning that produced it.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Next Action" icon="arrow-right">
    One specific thing to do. Not a list of considerations — one action that
    moves the work forward from the verdict.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Continuation Point" icon="bookmark">
    A clear point to resume from. You don't lose context between review cycles,
    and the next session knows exactly where to pick up.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Submit your work to the protocol">
    Paste your plan, artifact, or stuck decision into a session running the
    PA-PVP protocol. The clearer the input, the sharper the verdict.
  </Step>
  <Step title="First AI runs an adversarial review">
    The first AI reviews the submission specifically looking for gaps, weak
    logic, missing constraints, and assumptions that don't hold. It is not
    trying to improve the work — it is trying to break it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Second AI challenges the review">
    A second AI challenges both the original submission and the first review.
    This surfaces places where the adversarial critique was itself weak,
    unfair, or missed the actual problem.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Protocol synthesizes the final output">
    PA-PVP synthesizes a verdict from the adversarial cycle, identifies one
    next action, and marks a clean continuation point for the next session.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## When to Use PA-PVP

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Stuck decisions that keep generating discussion">
    If a decision has been reviewed multiple times and you still don't have a
    clear answer, PA-PVP forces one. The adversarial structure is designed
    specifically to break the "more discussion" loop.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Plans that need stress-testing before execution">
    Run a plan through PA-PVP before committing resources. The adversarial
    review finds the weak points that a supportive review would smooth over.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="AI-generated artifacts that need adversarial review">
    AI outputs tend to be internally coherent but brittle under challenge.
    PA-PVP stress-tests the artifact rather than accepting its internal logic
    at face value.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Work that needs a clear go/no-go verdict">
    When you need a real decision point — not more considerations — PA-PVP
    delivers a verdict you can act on.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
