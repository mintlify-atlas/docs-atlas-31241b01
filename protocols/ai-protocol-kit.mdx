The ai-protocol-kit is a curated set of behavior contracts for AI-assisted work. It is not a framework — there's no runtime, no installation, and no configuration file. Each contract is a text-based protocol you load into an AI session to define how the AI should interpret and respond to your inputs. The kit covers the most common places where AI-assisted workflows break: shaping raw ideas, reviewing outputs, preparing materials, writing for actual humans, and keeping sessions stable across longer work.

## What's in the Kit

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Idea Shaping">
    Protocols for turning raw ideas into structured starting points. These
    contracts help the AI ask useful questions, surface hidden assumptions, and
    produce a shaped artifact rather than a raw brainstorm dump. Use them when
    you have something half-formed and need it to become workable before you
    commit to building it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Work Review">
    Contracts for structured review of AI-generated content. Rather than asking
    the AI to "check this," these protocols define specific review criteria the
    AI applies — coverage, logic, gaps, and output quality — and produce a
    reviewable result rather than general feedback.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Page and Repository Preparation">
    Protocols for preparing materials before AI work begins. These contracts
    help structure pages, READMEs, or repository files so the AI has a clear
    scope, source boundary, and output target before it touches anything.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Writing for Real Readers">
    Contracts that keep AI writing aimed at actual humans. These protocols
    suppress filler, hedging, and generic tone — the output should read like
    something written for a specific person with a specific problem, not a
    content template.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Workflow Stability">
    Protocols that prevent AI-assisted workflows from breaking in common ways.
    These contracts cover session drift, context collapse, scope creep, and the
    gradual degradation that happens when a long session loses its original
    frame of reference.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How to Use the Kit

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify the type of work you're doing">
    Match the current task to one of the contract categories: are you shaping
    an idea, reviewing something, preparing materials, writing for a reader, or
    trying to keep a workflow stable?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the matching contract">
    Open the repository and locate the contract for your task type. Each
    contract is a self-contained text document — read it before loading it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Load it as the session baseline">
    Paste or reference the contract at the start of your AI session. This
    sets the behavioral contract before any real work begins.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run your work under the contract">
    Work normally. The contract shapes how the AI interprets your inputs and
    what it optimizes for in its responses.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review output against the contract's criteria">
    When the session produces output, use the contract's own criteria to assess
    it. If the output wouldn't pass its own contract, that's a clear signal to
    revise.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  These are text-based behavior contracts, not code. They work by shaping how
  the AI interprets and responds to your inputs — no installation, no
  dependencies, no runtime.
</Note>
